  • In the second part of the experiment, only the 7 voters of each group will have to make a decision. Candidates will just have to wait for continuation of the experiment.
  • Each voter of each group will receive an amount of points equal to 100. Each voter will have to choose how many of these 100 points to keep and how many to invest in a common project.
  • The points each voter decides to invest in the common project will be doubled and will then equally distributed among the other 6 voters of the group, excluding the voter himself.
  • Similarly, the amount of points invested by each voter of the group, for example by subject A, will be doubled and then distributed equally between all the other 6 voters of the group, excluding subject A.
    • How payoffs are determined
  • The payoffs you earn in the Part 2 will be equal to:
    100 points minus the amount of points you decide to invest in the group project plus plus the amount of points invested in the project by the other voters of your group . multiplied by 2 and divided by 6.
  • Example 1
    Suppose that a participant has decided to invest half of the total amount of points in the group project, i.e. 50 points. Suppose further that the other voters of his group have invested a total of 300 points in the group project. The payoff for this participant will be:
    100 - 50 + (300 * 2 / 6) = 150
  • Example 2
    Suppose that a participant has decided not to invest any of the total amount of points for the period in the group project, i.e. 0 points. Suppose further that the other voters of his group invested a total of 300 points in the project. The payoff for this participant will be:
    100 - 0 + (300 * 2 / 6) = 200
