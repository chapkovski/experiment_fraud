• Once both candidate A and candidate B have decided whether to implement electoral fraud or not, they will be mutually informed about it. Differently, members of party ALPHA and BETA will never be informed about whether candidate A or candidate B actually decided to implement electoral fraud or not.
  • Hereafter, each decides which of the following messages to send to all members of both Party ALPHA and Party BETA.
  • One of the following messages is chosen by candidate A:
  • - Candidate B decided to implement electoral fraud so to subtract one vote from Party ALPHA.
  • - Candidate B decided NOT to implement electoral fraud so to subtract one vote from Party ALPHA
  • - Nothing to communicate
  • One of the following messages is chosen by candidate B
  • - Candidate A decided to implement electoral fraud so to subtract one vote from Party BETA
  • - Candidate A decided NOT to implement electoral fraud so to subtract one vote from Party BETA
  • - Nothing to communicate
  • Finally, after all members of Party ALPHA and BETA have received both the message of Candidate A and of Candidate B, every party member will made a choice between voting or abstaining. At the end of each round both candidates A and B, as well as members of Party ALPHA and BETA will see which party has the highest number of votes as well as their own payoff for that round.
