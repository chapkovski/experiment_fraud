• Now we are explaining you the detailed instructions for the first part of the experiment. The first part consists of 5 rounds.
  • At the beginning of the first part you will be randomly and anonymously assigned to groups composed by 11 individuals each. The group composition will remain the same for all the 5 rounds of the first part of the experiment. You will never know the identity of the other members of your group, nor during the experiment, nor at the end of it.
  • Within each group composed by 11 participants, there will be the ALPHA party, composed by 4 individuals, and a Candidate A; the party BETA, composed by 5 individuals, and a candidate B.
    The role of Candidate A and Candidate B are randomly assigned at the beginning of the first part and will remain the same for all the 5 rounds of the first part;
    The role of party member of Party ALPHA and of Party BETA will be randomly assigned at the beginning of each round and thus might be different from round to round.
  • At the beginning of each round you will be informed about which party you are randomly assigned to or whether you are randomly assigned the role of Candidate A or Candidate B.
  • Moreover, to each member of Party ALPHA and of Party BETA it will be also randomly assigned a bonus Y. We will explain what this means in a moment.
  • After that, each member of Party ALPHA and of Party BETA will be asked to choose one option out of two: option “VOTE” or option “ABSENTION”. In order to make a choice you just have to select one of the two options and click on the “OK” button on your screen, as shown in the following screenshot:
  • After every party member has made a choice between voting or abstaining, the earnings for each member of Party ALPHA and Party BETA, as well as the earnings for Candidate A and Candidate B will be shown on the screen.

  • However, before each member of party ALPHA and BETA decides to vote or to abstain, both candidate A and candidate B have the opportunity to subtract a vote to the other party so to add it to their own party. The cost of electoral fraud is randomly selected so that it may vary from round to round. The cost of electoral fraud is the same for candidate A and for candidate B and it might be equal either to 20 points or to 40 points. In each period, all party members and candidates will be informed about the cost of electoral fraud.
  • Let’s make some examples:
  • Suppose that candidate A decides to implement electoral fraud so to subtract a vote to party BETA in order to add it to party ALPHA. Differently, candidate B decides not to implement electoral fraud.
  • -If party BETA has at least one member who decides to vote then this vote will be automatically subtracted from party BETA and added to party ALPHA
  • - If no member of party BETA decides to vote then a vote will be automatically added to party ALPHA while for party BETA nothing changes. 
  • Suppose that both candidate A and candidate B decide to implement electoral fraud. In this case nothing changes.
  • Once both candidate A and candidate B have decided whether to implement electoral fraud or not and they’ve been mutually informed about it, each candidate decides which of the following messages to send to all members of both Party Alpha and Party BETA.
  • One of the following messages is chosen by candidate A:
  • - Candidate B decided to implement electoral fraud so to subtract one vote from Party ALPHA.
  • - Candidate B decided NOT to implement electoral fraud so to subtract one vote from Party ALPHA
  • - Nothing to communicate
  • One of the following messages is chosen by candidate B
  • - Candidate A decided to implement electoral fraud so to subtract one vote from Party BETA
  • - Candidate A decided NOT to implement electoral fraud so to subtract one vote from Party BETA
  • - Nothing to communicate
  • Finally, after all members of Party ALPHA and BETA have received both the message of Candidate A and of Candidate B, every party member will made a choice between voting or abstaining. The earnings for each member of Party ALPHA and Party BETA, as well the earnings for Candidate A and Candidate B will be then showed on the screen.
